# Week 2 Analysis

## Learning points

The team learned how to transform a general product idea into structured requirements using user stories and prioritization techniques. Writing user stories helped us focus on the needs of real users rather than implementation details. The MoSCoW forced us to distinguish between essential functionality and desirable future features.

Creating the Figma prototype helped us understand the user journey and identify the screens required for the first version of the product and improve our skills in designing using Figma.

Developing MVP v0 demonstrated the importance of deploying a working product foundation early. Developing helped us to identify an inconvenience of using our app. For example, we decided to remove some tabs or redesign them to reduce the number of useless actions while using the app (e.g using calendar).

Customer underlined defects in our design (e.g redesign home page) and advised to write cjm and improve our design according to the this.

## Validated assumptions

- We assumed adding clothing items through photos would be one of the core product functions. This was confirmed.
- We assumed that the user do need the separate page for basket - rejected during the customer meeting. He said, that we do not need separate page for this.
- We assumed that the home page will be the wardrobe tab itself - rejected during the customer meeting. He said, that we need separate page for this.

## Needs clarification

- The exact technical solution for automatic background removal still needs investigation.
- The database structure and long-term storage strategy have not been finalized.
- Requirements for sharing outfits through links require further discussion with the customer.
- Non-functional requirements such as performance, scalability, and privacy requirements need further clarification.

## Planned response

Based on the feedback received during Week 2, the team will continue focusing on the selected MVP v1 scope:

- US-01: Telegram Authentication
- US-02: Add Clothing Item with Photo
- US-08: Automatic Background Removal
- US-04: Tags for Clothing Items
- US-05: Capsule Wardrobe

Team needs to:

- Define acceptance criteria for all MVP stories.
- Design and implement a database for storing wardrobe items.
- Research and select a suitable background-removal solution.
- Improve the prototype based on customer feedback.
- Expand MVP v0 into a functional MVP v1 implementation.
- Continue validating future features such as capsules, editing, and AI-assisted metadata detection before committing them to the implementation roadmap
